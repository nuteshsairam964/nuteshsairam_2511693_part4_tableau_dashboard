\# Chart Selection Justification



\## Dashboard Objective



The dashboard was designed to provide business managers with a clear understanding of sales performance, profitability, customer behavior, regional performance, shipping efficiency, discounts, and product returns. Each visualization was selected to answer a specific business question and support data-driven decision-making.



\---



\# 1. Sales Trend (Line Chart)



\### Business Question

How have sales changed over time?



\### Why a Line Chart?



A line chart is the most effective visualization for displaying trends over time. It clearly shows increases, decreases, and seasonal fluctuations in sales, allowing managers to identify growth patterns and monitor business performance.



\---



\# 2. Regional Performance (Map)



\### Business Question

Which geographic regions generate the highest sales and profit?



\### Why a Map?



A map provides an intuitive geographic representation of business performance. It enables managers to quickly identify high-performing and low-performing states and supports regional planning and resource allocation.



\---



\# 3. Category Profitability (Bar Chart)



\### Business Question

Which product categories generate the highest profit?



\### Why a Bar Chart?



A bar chart is ideal for comparing profitability across discrete categories. It allows users to quickly identify the strongest and weakest performing product categories.



\---



\# 4. Customer Segment (Bar Chart)



\### Business Question

Which customer segments contribute the most to business performance?



\### Why a Bar Chart?



Bar charts make comparisons between customer segments simple and easy to interpret. They clearly highlight differences in sales and profit across customer groups.



\---



\# 5. Shipping Performance (Bar Chart)



\### Business Question

Which shipping methods are most frequently used and how do they perform?



\### Why a Bar Chart?



Bar charts effectively compare operational performance across shipping methods and help identify opportunities to improve delivery efficiency.



\---



\# 6. Discount vs Profit (Scatter Plot)



\### Business Question

How do discounts affect profitability?



\### Why a Scatter Plot?



A scatter plot is the best choice for examining the relationship between two numerical variables. It helps identify whether higher discounts are associated with lower profits and reveals potential outliers.



\---



\# 7. Return Analysis (Bar Chart)



\### Business Question

How do product returns impact business performance?



\### Why a Bar Chart?



Bar charts allow clear comparison of returned and non-returned orders across categories or regions, helping identify areas with higher return rates.



\---



\# 8. KPI Cards



\### Business Question

What is the current overall business performance?



\### Why KPI Cards?



KPI cards provide an immediate summary of critical business metrics such as Total Sales, Total Profit, and Total Orders. They allow executives to assess business performance at a glance.



\---



\# Dashboard Filters



The dashboard includes interactive filters for State, Category, and Customer Segment. These filters allow users to dynamically explore the data, compare performance across different dimensions, and focus on specific business areas.



\---



\# Dashboard Interaction



The Regional Performance map was configured as an interactive filter. Selecting a state automatically updates the remaining dashboard visualizations, enabling users to perform detailed regional analysis without creating separate reports.



\---



\# Overall Justification



The dashboard combines line charts, bar charts, maps, scatter plots, KPI cards, and interactive filters to present multiple perspectives of business performance. This combination enables executives and business analysts to monitor trends, compare categories, identify geographic opportunities, evaluate operational efficiency, and support strategic decision-making using a single interactive dashboard.
